Classic Lasagna

Description

This classic lasagna recipe is a family favorite, featuring layers of tender pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy ricotta mixture, and melted mozzarella cheese. The recipe has been perfected over generations, balancing flavors and textures for the ultimate comfort food experience. Perfect for Sunday dinners or when you need to feed a crowd.

The key to this lasagna is in the slow-simmered meat sauce and the proper layering technique. Don't rush the sauce - the longer it simmers, the better the flavor. Let the assembled lasagna rest for 15 minutes after baking to allow the layers to set for perfect slices.

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C). Lightly grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. Cook lasagna noodles according to package directions until al dente. Drain and lay flat on a sheet of aluminum foil to prevent sticking.
  3. In a large skillet or Dutch oven, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and saut√© until translucent (about 5 minutes). Add garlic and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add ground beef and Italian sausage, breaking it up with a wooden spoon as it cooks. Cook until no pink remains (about 8-10 minutes). Drain excess fat if needed.
  5. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Reduce heat to low and simmer uncovered for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  6. In a medium bowl, mix ricotta cheese with egg and 1/4 cup of the Parmesan cheese until well combined.
  7. To assemble: Spread 1 cup of meat sauce in the bottom of the prepared baking dish. Arrange 4 noodles lengthwise over the sauce. Spread with 1/3 of the ricotta mixture, then 1 cup of meat sauce, and sprinkle with 1 cup mozzarella cheese. Repeat layers twice, ending with noodles, sauce, and the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan.
  8. Cover with foil (spray the underside with cooking spray to prevent sticking) and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake additional 25 minutes until bubbly and golden.
  9. Let stand for 15 minutes before serving to allow the layers to set.

Chef's Notes

Make Ahead: Assemble the lasagna up to 1 day in advance and refrigerate until ready to bake. You may need to add 10-15 minutes to the baking time if starting from cold.

Freezing: Baked lasagna freezes well for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in refrigerator before reheating.

Variations: Try adding saut√©ed mushrooms or spinach to the ricotta mixture, or substitute half the meat with Italian turkey sausage for a lighter version.
